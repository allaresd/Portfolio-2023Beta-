Mobile APP:
GoSport (2022)

Type

Educational digital mockup

Duration

2022.1-2022.4

Roles

Content Producer

Product Manager

Context

An educational digital mockup practice that helps Vancouverites simplify the online reservation process of sports venues(compared to the website version) and improves the user experience with a mobile app.

Project description

This project aims at understanding the basic concept of agile development as well as the digital business proposal by giving a specific scenario. Our team proposes to solve this problem by designing GoSport, a sports venue reservation app that covers the entire process of booking courts and focuses on pain points and scenarios. The expected results of the project include a substantial increase in the efficiency of venue reservations, higher user satisfaction, and more visitors to sports venues.

Tools

Figma, Miro, Zoom, Whatsapp, AE, Jianying, Google Drive

Key takeaways

Things that I will keep:

User-centric design. We conducted two rounds of user tests to validate if the product pillars are appealing to users and if the process is easy to navigate. Fortunately, we obtained useful information by conducting clear and feasible user test plans.

Multiple media explanation. To create understandable information, such as a pitch deck or a product instruction, using illustrations, videos, and animations is vital for the audience as the majority are not interested in the long typed page.

Things that I will do differently:

Overcome the challenges of designing a regional mobile app as a newcomer. As a newcomer, designing a regional mobile app from scratch is quite tricky. For one thing, I find it hard to identify the user’s pain points due to the lack of long presence. For another, we may not be clear about local regulations that meet the standard of compliance when designing a mobile app that might collect users’ information. Therefore, next time I need more field investigation to collaborate with more stakeholders and do more first-hand user research to observe users’ behaviours in person.

Be more agile. Agile methodology is an effective way to respond, change, and iterate for stakeholders in the process of software development. For this project, we were not so agile because we didn't break down the sprints into small chunks. Therefore, some teammates may not be clear about tasks and may have repetitive work. In addition, I realize that agile is not just about following basic rules (like Scrum: Backlog-Sprint-Standup-Review-Retrospective) but also about making fast decisions. Next, teamwork needs strict time-box decisions to ensure they can be confirmed by everyone and push the project forward in a timely manner.
